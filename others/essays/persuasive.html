Video Game as a Form of Art

Art is produced by human imagination and creative skills. It is made to be appreciated by its beauty, its story, its message or its emotional property. Commonly known constituents of the arts are literary arts: like poems and novels, visual arts: like paintings and architecture, and performing arts: like music and dance. But where do video games fall under? The question of whether video games are worthy enough to be considered as an art form is still a controversial topic. Video games exhibit imagination and creative skills not just by graphics but even by its story telling and music which attest they are an art form.

The fact that video games comprise other forms of art supports this argument. “Video games have become an amalgam of traditional art forms -- and have become one in its own right”, argues Chris Melissinos, a gaming expert and the author of “The Art of Video Games: From Pac-Man to Mass Effect”. Starting with a game’s graphic design which is usually what the user or the player will initially notice, Chris Melissinos mentioned forms of traditional artistic expression that are involved in its making like – “sculpture in the form of 3D modeling and illustration”. The texts on the title screen, the characters, the worlds, and even the smallest objects that make up a video game are drawn, painted, textured and enhanced through digital tools, or in some cases, are even hand-crafted and are digitized in order to be imported as part of the game’s art design. This process of creating these elements of a video game demonstrates the artistic skills and talent of the individual or the people behind its design.

Another aspect in which meaningful and engaging experiences will be encountered is in the video games’ storytelling. Games like Assassins Creed, Prince of Persia and Tomb Raider, have film adaptations because of their rich and fascinating narratives. A video game’s story development is challenging because they’re interactive. The players are part of the experience so game designers must aspire for high level of entertainment and emotional involvement. And this is what makes video games unique. The story of the author bound by the rules of the game is followed by the players, which allows them immersion in how the story would flow, making the experience utterly personal. And “If you can observe the work of another and find in it personal connection, then art has been achieved”, said Chris Melissinos.

Contradictory, a film critic, Roger Ebert, gained a lot of attention in the mid-2000s when he made a claim that “video games can never be art”. His claim ignited a controversy across the gaming and art community. He stated, “To my knowledge, no one in or out of the field has ever been able to cite a game worthy of comparison with the great dramatists, poets, filmmakers, novelists and composers. That a game can aspire to artistic importance as a visual experience, I accept. But for most gamers, video games represent a loss of those precious hours we have available to make ourselves more cultured, civilized and empathetic.” Ebert’s very own statement displays irony when he said that he accepts a game to aspire to artistic importance as a visual experience. Art works, as Google suggested, are “produced to be appreciated for their beauty or emotional power”. The visual experience video games offer, with the addition of engaging storylines, profound dialogues and revered music, are synonymous to what art should be recognized for. Furthermore, common misconceptions about video games include the descriptions that Roger Ebert used to account the loss of precious hours most gamers could have rather used for. Most gamers are often assumed to be anti-socials. However, because of the internet and with some games no longer playable offline, gaming is already being viewed as a social activity which contributes to social development and the empathy of its users.

The video game industry is rapidly growing and evolving. Could it be that these electronic games are so dynamic that it had actually gone beyond the conventional properties of art for most people to see them as its form? Games are meant to engage its users in its story, or to take you to a whole new virtual dimension while listening to its music that invoke wide range of emotions in a way that no other medium could, which is why art predecessors discern games as interlopers of the art world. These games immerse you to an interactive experience that is the result of its creators’ expression of their creativity and imagination.
