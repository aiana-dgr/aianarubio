Money and Happiness

Modern society today could not function without money. It is the current medium of exchange. Economies rely on the exchange of money for products and services. But what is the role of money in creating happiness? Can money make you happy? Or is it true, what they say, that “Money can’t buy happiness.”? Money can make us happy because life without it is really hard. There are many ways it can make us happy.

To start with, it is easy to understand that people who have enough to cover rent or housing, food and clothing would most likely be happier than people who struggle to pay their bills. Knowing that we have enough money to spend on our basic necessities gives us peace of mind. And real happiness is borne out of a peaceful mind.

Some may say that once you’re able to provide for your basic needs, money and material items are a lot less likely to make you happy. However, having security allows the wealthy to explore more active ways of experiencing the world. Money plays a bigger role in our lives and in the society in variety of ways. Such as in business, money helps people achieve a larger chance of business success. Even in education, money allows people achieve a higher quality of education. People value education highly in their lives and a quality education brings oneself a higher possibility to succeed in the future. These achievements generate greater happiness, satisfaction and joy.

Additionally, money opens more opportunities for people to give and to help others. A study by Molly and Grafman in 2006, showed the biological implications of “altruistic behaviour”, or the behaviour that show a desire to help other people, in brain scans. In their study, “The anterior prefrontal cortex lit up in scans of brains of the altruistic.” This shows that people get a feeling of great happiness and excitement from the act of giving. So, spend money on others. Giving money or gifts strengthens social bonds, which amplify happiness.

In conclusion, money does play a role in creating happiness. Living in a financially stable home improves your happiness. Having enough money to live on can ease anxiety, it refrains you from worrying and allows you to enjoy life. The feeling of security that wealth gives is the key to happiness. Life satisfaction, sense of worth and happiness can grow higher, as the level of wealth increases, as long as you keep in mind how to spend it right.
